## Key findings

1. **Root cause is missing augmentation, not composition complexity.**
   `single_atom_all10` (no composition) is also near-random (+2.4pp best).
   Joint objectives (B/C/D) have no augmentation in fixed-data mode: they
   re-show the same `(x1,x2,x3)` triplets every epoch and memorise sample
   identities after 2–6 epochs instead of learning PID structure.

2. **Model A (unimodal, has VectorAugmenter) is the most stable.**
   Best epoch 34–49 vs 2–6 for joint models. Drift ≈ +0.05 vs +0.4–0.9.
   Yet even A barely exceeds random on all-10 experiments,
   suggesting that augmentation alone is not sufficient at 10k samples.

3. **Family-restricted experiments work marginally.**
   Unique: B reaches +8.2pp above random (41.5% vs 33.3%).
   Redundancy: A reaches +4.9pp. Synergy: C reaches +5.0pp.
   The easier 3–4 class task and smaller dataset help, but gains are modest.

4. **The fix: add augmentation to joint fixed-data training.**
   Apply `VectorAugmenter` to `(x1,x2,x3)` inside `_compute_trimodal_ssl_loss`
   (same jitter_std=0.08, feat_drop=0.08, gain 0.92–1.08 as model A).
   This should eliminate memorisation and allow joint objectives to train
   stably through many epochs, as in the streaming training regime.

5. **TRIANGLE (C) shows the smallest overfitting drift** across all experiments,
   making it the most robust joint objective in this regime.
